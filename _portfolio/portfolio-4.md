As Lead Architect, I designed and built the AgWeatherNet mobile app from the ground up using Flutter, driving system design, development, deployment, and feature evolution across iOS and Android throughout the full product lifecycle. The app has reached 4K+ downloads across platforms since launch.

### What It Delivers

The app gives growers instant access to real-time weather observations from hundreds of AgWeatherNet stations across Washington State, including temperature, humidity, wind speed, and precipitation pulled live from AgWeatherNet's sensor network, with both current readings and historical trends available per station.

An interactive station map lets growers locate the nearest monitoring sites and explore station-level data across the region. Integrated agricultural decision tools help translate weather data into actionable guidance covering irrigation scheduling, frost protection timing, and spray condition windows, reducing the gap between raw sensor readings and on-farm decisions.

### Architecture and Design Decisions

Building in Flutter allowed a single codebase to serve both iOS and Android with full native performance, a key requirement given AgWeatherNet's grower base spans both platforms. The app is architected for reliability in low-connectivity environments: data caching strategies ensure core functionality holds up in areas with spotty cell coverage, which is common in agricultural settings across rural Washington.

The interface is deliberately straightforward, designed for growers of all technical backgrounds who need fast answers in the field rather than feature-dense dashboards that require a learning curve.
