The AgWeatherNet mobile app brings the full power of WSU's AgWeatherNet platform to growers' hands, providing instant access to real-time weather observations from hundreds of stations across Washington State. As Lead Architect, I drove the system design, development, and feature evolution of the app from the ground up using Flutter, ensuring a consistent and performant experience across iOS and Android.

The app delivers current and historical weather data — including temperature, humidity, wind speed, and precipitation — pulled live from AgWeatherNet's sensor network. An interactive station map lets users locate the nearest monitoring sites, while integrated agricultural decision tools help growers make timely, data-driven choices about irrigation, frost protection, and spray scheduling.

Built for reliability in the field, the app is optimized for low-connectivity environments and designed with a clean, accessible interface suited to growers of all technical backgrounds.
