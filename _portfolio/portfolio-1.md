I led the mobile development of AWN CropAI as part of the AgWeatherNet team at WSU, an AI-powered app that helps growers manage fruit sunburn risk and monitor crop maturity across the growing season. AWN CropAI was recognized with the **ASABE AE50 Award for Outstanding Innovations in Product or Systems Technology (2026)**, one of the most competitive honors in agricultural engineering.

### Heat Stress Monitoring

By connecting a thermal-RGB camera to the app, growers can capture thermal images of fruit canopies and instantly assess Fruit Surface Temperature (FST). The app identifies real-time heat stress levels and provides a 7-day FST forecast powered by ML models trained on large-scale AgWeatherNet station data, with hourly projections to help plan ahead for heat events. Built-in controls including fruit diameter customization and wind speed overlays let growers fine-tune predictions for their specific orchard conditions.

### Color and Maturity Analysis

AWN CropAI also performs color analysis on captured fruit images, estimating the percentage of red, yellow, and green across each fruit. This gives growers an objective measure of maturity and quality, supporting decisions around harvest timing, netting removal, and post-harvest handling. Result screens display sunburn risk severity alongside color distribution in a single view.

### Design Approach

The app is built to be practical in the field, with an intuitive interface that lets growers select crops and cultivars, adjust weather inputs, and interpret results without any technical background. The architecture handles real-time image processing on-device while pulling live forecast data from AgWeatherNet's backend, keeping the experience responsive even in low-connectivity orchard environments.
